# Appendix A — why the learning signal is maximized at p = 0.5

Setup. A verifier returns a binary reward `r ∈ {0,1}`. For a fixed prompt at a
given difficulty, let `p = Pr[r = 1]` be the policy's success probability. We
draw a group of `G` i.i.d. rollouts; let `k = Σ_j r_j ∼ Binomial(G, p)` be the
number correct.

### A.1 GRPO advantages vanish on zero-variance groups
GRPO/DAPO sets the advantage of rollout `j` to
`A_j = (r_j − r̄) / (σ_r + ε)`, with `r̄ = k/G` and
`σ_r² = (1/G) Σ_j (r_j − r̄)² = (k/G)(1 − k/G) = p̂(1−p̂)`.
If `k = 0` or `k = G` then `σ_r = 0` and every `A_j = 0`: the group produces **no
policy gradient**. DAPO encodes this by discarding such groups (dynamic
sampling). So the trainable rollouts are exactly those in *informative* groups
with `0 < k < G`.

### A.2 Probability a group is informative
```
P_inf(p) = Pr[0 < k < G] = 1 − Pr[k=0] − Pr[k=G] = 1 − (1−p)^G − p^G .
```
Differentiate: `P_inf'(p) = G(1−p)^{G−1} − G p^{G−1} = 0`
⇒ `(1−p)^{G−1} = p^{G−1}` ⇒ `p = 1−p` ⇒ **`p* = 1/2`**.
Second derivative is negative there, so `p = 0.5` is the unique maximizer for all
`G ≥ 2`. (E.g. `G=8`: `P_inf(0.5) ≈ 0.992` vs `P_inf(0.9) ≈ 0.57`.)

### A.3 Expected within-group reward variance
The population reward variance is `Var[r] = p(1−p)`, maximized at `p = 0.5`
(`d/dp [p − p²] = 1 − 2p = 0`). The expected empirical variance
`E[p̂(1−p̂)] = p(1−p)(1 − 1/G)` is a positive multiple of it, so it shares the
maximizer. Thus both the *frequency* of informative groups and the *magnitude*
of the advantages they carry peak at `p = 0.5`.

### A.4 Consequence for difficulty control
Because per-environment success `p` is a monotone-decreasing function of the
difficulty level `d`, there exists a difficulty `d*` with `p(d*) = 0.5` that
maximizes the expected learning signal. RLVE's rule raises the difficulty ceiling
only once `p ≥ τ_acc = 0.9 > p*`, i.e. it keeps the model in a regime of low
signal (`P_inf(0.9) ≪ P_inf(0.5)`) and never lowers difficulty if the model
regresses. **FEP-RLVE** (Appendix B) instead *selects* `d` by minimizing an
expected-free-energy objective whose pragmatic term `U(d)` peaks at `p(d)=0.5`,
so the sampled difficulties concentrate near the signal-optimal band — while a
second, epistemic term lets the controller actively probe the model's evolving
competence and move difficulty *both* up and down. For non-binary/shaped rewards the optimum shifts away from 0.5 (it
maximizes `Var[r]` under the reward distribution), and `p*` becomes a tunable
target; we use `p* = 0.5` for the binary-correctness reward.

# Appendix B — active-inference (FEP) formulation of difficulty control

> **Note.** This is the Friston *Free Energy Principle* formulation, **not** a
> thermodynamic "energy − entropy" objective. Difficulty selection is cast as
> *active inference*: a generative model of competence, **variational** free
> energy for belief update (perception), and **expected** free energy for action
> (which difficulty to train on next). Appendix A's informative-group utility
> `U_K(p)=1−p^K−(1−p)^K` reappears here as the *preference* over outcomes.

### B.1 Generative model (hidden competence → outcome)
For each environment `e`, let `s_e ∈ ℝ` be the model's latent **competence** and
`d` a difficulty level. The outcome `o ∈ {0,1}` (verifier: correct/wrong) is
generated by an IRT-style likelihood
```
P(o = 1 | s_e, d) = σ( a · (s_e − d) ),
```
with discrimination `a`. The agent (the trainer) maintains a **belief**
`q(s_e)` over competence. Predicted success at difficulty `d` is
```
p̂(d) = E_{s∼q}[ σ(a(s − d)) ]  =  q(o = 1 | d).
```

### B.2 Perception = variational free energy minimization (belief update)
Given a batch of observed outcomes `{(d, o)}`, the variational free energy
```
F[q] = E_{q(s)}[ ln q(s) − ln p(o, s) ]  =  D_KL( q(s) ‖ p(s|o) ) − ln p(o)
```
is minimized (over `q`) by the Bayesian posterior. On a competence grid this is
the exact update
```
q(s) ← q(s) · ∏_k P(o_k | s, d_k) / Z .
```
This — *not* an entropy-regularized energy — is the "free energy" being
minimized in the perception step. It is implemented verbatim in
`FEPRLVEController.observe()`.

### B.3 Action = expected free energy (which difficulty to train on)
To choose the next difficulty, active inference minimizes the **expected free
energy** `G_e(d)`, which decomposes into a pragmatic (preference) and an
epistemic (information-gain) term, plus an optional cost:
```
G_e(d) = E_{q(o|d)}[ − ln P_pref(o) ]     (pragmatic / preference risk)
         − I_q(s_e ; o | d)               (epistemic / information gain)
         + λ_cost · C(e, d).              (rollout/verify cost, optional)
```

**Pragmatic term.** The preference `P_pref` is over *outcomes*, and crucially is
**not** "always correct" (that would prefer easy tasks). We prefer outcomes that
carry a usable gradient — a group of `K` rollouts that is *informative*
(`0 < Σ_k o_k < K`), whose predicted probability is exactly `U_K(p̂(d))`. Taking
the preference proportional to that informative-group probability, the
preference-risk term is
```
E_{q(o|d)}[ − ln P_pref(o) ] = − ln U_K(p̂(d)),
U_K(p) = 1 − p^K − (1−p)^K   (Appendix A; maximal at p = 0.5),
```
so the pragmatic contribution to `G` is `+ λ_sig · (− ln U_K(p̂(d)))`. We use the
`− ln U_K` form (rather than a linear `− U_K`) because it is the literal
`− ln P_pref`, and because it **diverges as `U_K → 0`**: a near-degenerate
difficulty (all-pass or all-fail) carries risk `→ +∞` and is strongly repelled,
while the minimum sits at the `p = 0.5` signal-optimal band.

**Epistemic term.** The expected information gain about competence from observing
the outcome at difficulty `d` is the mutual information
```
I_q(s_e ; o | d) = H[q(s_e)] − E_{o∼q(o|d)} H[q(s_e | o, d)]
                 = H[Ber(p̂(d))] − E_{s∼q} H[Ber(σ(a(s−d)))]
```
(the two lines are equal by symmetry of MI). It is largest where outcomes are
most diagnostic of `s_e`, and — unlike a fixed `p=0.5` target — its location
depends on the current belief uncertainty. This is the term that lets the
controller *actively probe* the model's competence frontier.

### B.4 Policy = active-inference softmax over EFE
Active inference defines the policy prior as "lower expected free energy is more
probable", `P(π) ∝ exp(−G(π))`, so the difficulty policy is the softmax
```
π(d | e) ∝ exp( − G_e(d) / T ),     precision γ = 1/T.
```
This softmax is the *standard active-inference action-selection rule*; it is
**not** an externally added max-entropy/thermodynamic term. Limits: `T→0` is
greedy (pick `argmin_d G`), `T→∞` is uniform exploration.

### B.5 RLVE is a degenerate special case
RLVE's "raise difficulty when accuracy ≥ 0.9" rule is recovered by setting
`λ_info = 0` (no epistemic drive), `T → 0` (greedy), and using a preference for
*high accuracy* rather than for the *informative* (`p≈0.5`) band — a hard,
one-directional, threshold controller. FEP-RLVE relaxes all three into a single
differentiable, ablatable objective.

### B.6 Implementation
The belief `q(s_e)` is a per-environment grid (exact Bayes, no Gaussian/Laplace
approximation), updated by `observe()`; difficulties are scored by `G()` and
sampled by `policy()`. See `rlve_repro/active_inference_controller.py` (the EFE
controller) and `rlve_repro/active_inference_manager.py` (the SLIME wiring).
Ablations: `Signal-RLVE` sets `λ_info = 0` (pragmatic only); `FEP-RLVE` uses the
full EFE.
